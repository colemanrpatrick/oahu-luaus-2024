Visiting a Hawaiian luau is on the to-do list of many visitors to Oahu. This Hawaiian tradition is the perfect way to immerse yourself in local island culture, try Hawaiian delicacies and enjoy music and dances from Hawaii and other Polynesian islands.

Among the best Oahu luaus are, Aloha Kai Luau, Experience Nutridge Luau, Toa Luau and Ali'i Luau.. These are the luaus that stand out with cultural presentations, a big all-you-care-to-eat luau buffet dinner and Hawaiian-style musical entertainment.

A Hawaiian luau offers the chance to sample popular luau foods, such as kalua roast pig, lomi lomi salmon, fresh island fish and poi, which is made from cooked and pounded taro root. Many Oahu luaus also include a variety of continental favorites and vegetarian options, so no one will leave hungry.

Prior to the big luau feast, all guests are invited to watch or participate in island arts & crafts and other activities, which may include flower lei or bookmark making, ukulele and hula lessons and traditional games.

Some Oahu luaus also include the traditional imu ceremony, in which the kalua pua'a (roast pig) is removed from the imu, the underground oven. All Oahu luaus culminate with a spectacular Polynesian show featuring music and dances from the Hawaiian Islands, as well as Greater Polynesia.
